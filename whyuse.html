Why should I use Pencil.Gaming?

Why Pencil.Gaming is superior to other managed gaming libraries

So you might wonder what makes Pencil.Gaming so special compared to other gaming libraries, like XNA/MonoGame, OpenTK or SharpDX. There are a number of reasons that Pencil.Gaming is superior to all of the previously mentioned libraries.

Firstly, you have to understand that I have used XNA and OpenTK in the past, and I don't think these libraries are necessarily terrible, I just think they have some fundamental flaws that Pencil.Gaming manages to resolve.

Pencil.Gaming is better than XNA because...

  • It's cross-platform
  • It's open-source/free
  • It's under active development
  • The end-user doesn't have to install redistributables

Pencil.Gaming is better than MonoGame because...

  • You don't have to feel dirty when using "Microsoft.Xna" namespaces
  • It's smaller, and therefore less prone to bugs
  • It's not constrained by the development pace of a dead library...

Pencil.Gaming is better than SharpDX because...

  • It's cross platform
  • And that's a big deal

Pencil.Gaming is better than OpenTK because...

  • It's under active development
  • Its windowing/input system relies on a well-established, solid, native library
  • There's no need for the end-user to install OpenAL
